# Skill: Brainstorm

## Purpose

Facilitate creative brainstorming sessions to explore product concepts, feature ideas, UX patterns, design directions, technical architecture options, and naming/branding ideas. This skill helps generate a wide range of possibilities before narrowing down to specific implementation decisions.

## When to use this skill

- You want to **explore multiple approaches** before choosing one.
- You need **creative ideas** for features, UX patterns, or design directions.
- You're starting a **new project** and want to think through the concept.
- You want to **evaluate trade-offs** between different technical or design choices.
- You need help with **naming, branding, or copy** ideas.
- You want to **challenge assumptions** and think outside the box.
- You're **stuck** and need fresh perspectives on a problem.

## Inputs

- **Problem statement**: What are you trying to solve or explore?
- **Constraints**: Budget, timeline, technical limitations, brand guidelines.
- **Inspiration**: Reference sites, competitors, design styles, or moods.
- **Target audience**: Who will use this? What do they care about?
- **Existing decisions**: What's already been decided (stack, brand, etc.)?

## Brainstorming modes

### 1. Feature brainstorm
Generate feature ideas for a product or section. Output as a prioritized list with brief rationale for each.

### 2. UX/UI exploration
Explore different UI patterns, layouts, and interaction models. Reference real-world examples and explain trade-offs.

### 3. Technical architecture
Compare frameworks, libraries, and architectural patterns. Produce a decision matrix with pros/cons/fit scores.

### 4. Design direction
Explore visual styles, color palettes, typography, and motion design approaches. Reference mood boards and design trends.

### 5. Copy and naming
Generate name ideas, taglines, CTAs, microcopy, and tone-of-voice options. Test against brand values.

### 6. Animation and motion
Brainstorm animation concepts for page transitions, micro-interactions, loading states, and ambient/background effects. Consider performance impact.

## Required behavior

1. **Generate quantity first**: Aim for 5-10+ ideas before evaluating.
2. **Defer judgment**: Present all ideas without premature filtering.
3. **Build on ideas**: Use "yes, and..." thinking to extend promising concepts.
4. **Consider extremes**: Include at least one bold/unconventional option.
5. **Ground in reality**: Tag each idea with feasibility (easy/medium/hard).
6. **Synthesize**: After generating, group ideas into themes and recommend top 3.
7. **Document decisions**: Record what was chosen and why for future reference.

## Output format

For each brainstorming session, produce:

1. **Ideas list**: Numbered list with brief description and feasibility tag.
2. **Themes**: Group related ideas into 3-5 themes.
3. **Top picks**: Recommend 2-3 ideas with rationale.
4. **Next steps**: Concrete actions to move forward with chosen ideas.
5. **Parking lot**: Good ideas that don't fit now but should be revisited.

## Integration with other skills

- After brainstorming, use **vibe-coding** to rapidly prototype the chosen concept.
- Use **product-management** to formalize chosen ideas into PRDs.
- Use **frontend-ui-integration** to implement specific UI decisions.

## Safety and escalation

- Brainstorming is exploration, not commitment. Clearly label ideas as exploratory.
- If a brainstorm reveals the project scope is much larger than expected, flag this early.
- Don't over-invest in brainstorming when the path is already clear -- move to implementation.
