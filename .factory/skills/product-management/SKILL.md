# Skill: Product management

## Purpose

Assist with core product management activities including writing product requirements documents (PRDs), analyzing feature requests, synthesizing user research, planning roadmaps, and communicating product decisions.

## When to use this skill

- You need to **write or update PRDs** with clear requirements, success metrics, and technical considerations.
- You're **evaluating feature requests** and need structured analysis.
- You need to **synthesize research** findings into actionable insights.
- You're **planning roadmaps** and need to organize and prioritize.
- You need to **communicate product decisions** to stakeholders.

## Conventions for Get Outta Here

### PRD structure
1. **Problem statement**: What user pain point or need?
2. **Goals and success metrics**: Quantitative success criteria.
3. **User stories**: Who uses this and how?
4. **Requirements**: Functional and non-functional, prioritized.
5. **Technical considerations**: Architecture, APIs, dependencies.
6. **Risks and mitigations**: What could go wrong?
7. **Launch plan**: Rollout strategy, monitoring.

### Feature prioritization
Use RICE framework: Reach x Impact x Confidence / Effort

## Required behavior

1. **Understand context deeply** before proposing changes.
2. **Be specific and actionable** with concrete, testable requirements.
3. **Consider tradeoffs** explicitly.
4. **Make it measurable** with concrete metrics.

## Integration with other skills

- Use **brainstorm** for ideation before formalizing into PRDs.
- Use **vibe-coding** to rapidly prototype features from PRDs.
- Use **frontend-ui-integration** to implement specific features.
